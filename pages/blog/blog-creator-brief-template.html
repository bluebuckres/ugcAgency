The Creator Brief Template That Gets Results

UGC Agency Team December 12, 2024 8 min read
Marketing Tactics Creator Management

A great creator brief is the difference between mediocre UGC and content that drives sales. Here's the exact template our team uses to brief 120+ creators every month.

Why Most Creator Briefs Fail

We've seen it all - brands that give creators a single sentence ("Just make it authentic!") and brands that write 5-page novels. Both approaches fail for different reasons:

‚ùå Too Vague

"Make authentic content about our skincare routine"

Results in generic, low-converting content

‚ùå Too Restrictive

Script every word, control every gesture

Kills authenticity and creator's unique voice

The Perfect Creator Brief Template

1. Campaign Overview (30 seconds to understand)

üìã Template:

  • Brand: [Brand Name]
  • Product: [Specific product + key benefit]
  • Goal: [Drive awareness/sales/sign-ups]
  • Target Audience: [Age, interests, pain points]
  • Platform: [TikTok/Instagram/Both + format]
  • Deadline: [Submission date]

2. Content Direction (The creative guardrails)

üéØ Template:

Content Type: [Unboxing/Tutorial/Before&After/Day-in-life]

Tone: [Excited discovery/Honest review/Educational/Lifestyle]

Key Message: "This [product] helps [target audience] [achieve benefit] without [common pain point]"

Hook Direction: [Give 2-3 hook options using our formula]

Must Include:

  • Product name mentioned within first 10 seconds
  • Key benefit demonstrated visually
  • Clear call-to-action

3. Product Information (Everything they need to know)

üõçÔ∏è Template:

Product Details:

  • What it is: [Simple explanation]
  • Key ingredients/features: [Top 3 only]
  • How to use: [Step-by-step if needed]
  • Price point: [If mentioning price is encouraged]

Unique Selling Points:

  • [USP #1 - why it's different]
  • [USP #2 - key benefit]
  • [USP #3 - social proof element]

4. Visual Guidelines (Show, don't just tell)

üì∏ Template:

Lighting: [Natural/bright indoor/specific mood]

Setting: [Bathroom/kitchen/bedroom/outdoors]

Outfit/Styling: [Casual/glam/workout wear]

Shots to Include:

  • Close-up of product packaging
  • Product being used/applied
  • Results/transformation (if applicable)

Brand Assets: [Link to logos, colors, fonts if needed]

5. Do's and Don'ts (Clear boundaries)

‚úÖ DO

  • Keep your authentic voice
  • Show genuine reactions
  • Use natural transitions
  • Include lifestyle context
  • Mention specific results

‚ùå DON'T

  • Use competitor products in frame
  • Make medical claims
  • Rush through the demonstration
  • Forget to include CTA
  • Use copyrighted music

6. Call-to-Action & Links

üîó Template:

Primary CTA: "[Specific action - shop now/try it/get yours]"

Link in Bio: [Specific URL or bio link]

Discount Code: [If applicable - CREATOR15]

Hashtags: [Brand hashtag + 3-5 relevant hashtags]

Pro Tips for Better Creator Briefs

üé® Include Visual References

Link to 2-3 examples of the style/tone you want. A picture is worth a thousand words in a brief.

üì± Platform-Specific Notes

TikTok vs Instagram require different approaches. Be specific about format, length, and style for each platform.

üîÑ Feedback Loop

Include your contact info and encourage questions. Better to clarify upfront than get disappointing results.

‚è∞ Timeline Clarity

Be clear about submission deadlines, revision timelines, and when content will go live.

Real Example: Beauty Brand Brief

üìã Glow Serum - TikTok UGC Brief

Goal: Drive sales for new vitamin C serum launch

Hook Options:

  • "Wait, this $25 serum gave me better results than my $150 one?"
  • "I was not expecting this budget skincare to work this well..."
  • "Okay but why is my skin literally glowing after just one week?"

Key Message: "This vitamin C serum gives you visible glow in 7 days without irritation"

Must Show: Before skin (close-up), applying serum, after skin (7 days later)

CTA: "Get yours with my code GLOW20 - link in my bio!"
