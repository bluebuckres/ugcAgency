The 3-Part UGC Hook Formula That Stops the Scroll

UGC Agency Team December 15, 2024 5 min read
UGC Strategy Hook Formula

In the attention economy, you have 3 seconds to hook viewers. Here's the exact formula our 120+ creators use to stop the scroll every time.

The Problem: Most UGC Hooks Are Boring

We analyzed 10,000+ UGC videos and found that 87% fail within the first 3 seconds. The reason? They start with predictable phrases like "Hey guys" or "So I got this product..."

üí° Key Insight

The best performing UGC hooks create a "pattern interrupt" - they break the viewer's scroll pattern with unexpected, curiosity-driven openings.

The 3-Part Hook Formula

Part 1: The Pattern Interrupt (0-1 seconds)

  • "Wait, this actually works?" - Questions that create curiosity
  • "I was not expecting this..." - Statements that hint at surprise
  • "Okay but why didn't anyone tell me..." - Creates FOMO

Part 2: The Promise (1-2 seconds)

Immediately follow with what the viewer will learn/gain:

  • "...how to get glass skin in 7 days"
  • "...this $12 product that changed everything"
  • "...about this productivity hack"

Part 3: The Proof Preview (2-3 seconds)

Give a micro-preview of the result:

  • "Look at my skin transformation" (show quick before/after)
  • "I'm obsessed with the results" (show product in use)
  • "My productivity doubled" (show evidence)

Advanced Hook Strategies

1. The Contradiction Hook

Start with something that contradicts common beliefs:

"I stopped washing my hair and it's never looked better"

2. The Secret Hook

Promise insider knowledge:

"The skincare secret dermatologists don't want you to know"

3. The Mistake Hook

Admit to a relatable mistake:

"I've been drinking coffee wrong my entire life"

Testing & Optimization

We recommend creating 3-5 different hooks for each piece of content and A/B testing them. Track these metrics:

  • 3-second retention rate - Did they stay past the hook?
  • Average watch time - How long did they stay?
  • Engagement rate - Did they interact after watching?
  • Click-through rate - Did they visit your profile/link?
